Failaka Island

Failaka Island is one of the Kuwaiti islands that are considered a very important commercial station located on the sea route that connects the civilizations of Mesopotamia, and its name is taken from the Greek word Felakio, meaning a point of concentration, and the island was one of the most important religious centers in ancient times, and it is divided administratively into Many villages, such as the village of Al-Qusour district in its center, the village of Saida on the northwestern side of it, the village of Dasht in the northeastern side of it, the village of Al-Qurainiya overlooking the sea, the village of Al-Zour, and the village of Al-Sabahiya, which is characterized by freshwater wells and palm trees.

Qaruh Island

The smallest southern Kuwaiti island and the most penetrated into the sea, as it is 37.5 km from the coast of Al-Zour, 33 km and 789 meters from Kibar Island, 16 km and 90 meters from Umm Al-Maradim Island, away from Awha Island, and Ras Mishaab in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia 40 nautical miles. It is 275 meters long, and in some places it does not exceed 175 meters.


Wildlife

The island inhabits many flocks of various seabirds, which lay their eggs among the weeds on their land, and some wild birds also visit them in the spring, and grass grows on its land after the rains, and there are some annual plants in it, and some shrubs are scattered in it that resist salinity and drought, as well. On its territory there are some reptiles, lizards and some environmental insects, and the island's beaches contain seashells that contain marine animals with soft bodies


The dangers that the island face

On September 26, 2007, a report was published that the island of Qaruh could be submerged due to pollution, as the island was surrounded by coral belts, but these belts began to break because of the boats mooring next to them, causing strong waves to reach the island that did not rise from the ground significantly. In 1997, the Director of the Environmental Sciences Department at the Scientific Research Institute Dr. Al-Ghadban said that there are two reasons that may lead to the disappearance of the island, the first is the disappearance of coral reefs and the second is the rise in sea levels. The change in the direction of the waves during the seasons of the year led to a very rapid reduction in the size of the island, so a marine pier was created that limits the waves and saves the island from the smallness of its land area and added many marine anchors around the island to moor boats to protect the coral reefs surrounding the island. Was done the island was declared a natural reserve in 2008, and the laws to protect wildlife have not been implemented on its soil yet.
