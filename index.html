Kuwait

The State of Kuwait is one of the countries of the Gulf Cooperation Council, located in the Arabian Peninsula region, specifically in the northwestern part of the Arabian Gulf, [2] within the periphery of the Middle East region. It is considered an independent Arab state and is distinguished by its large oil production; It possesses about 10% of the total proven oil reserves in the world, and it should be noted that the word (Kuwait) is a diminution of the word (Kot), which means: fort


The location and area of ​​Kuwait

The State of Kuwait is located in the Middle East region, and it has land and sea borders. As it overlooks from the eastern side the waters of the Arabian Gulf, and shares its western and northern borders with Iraq, and is bordered by the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia on the southern side. It is divided into 254 km with the Iraqi border, and 221 km with the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, as for its astronomical position, it lies within the latitude (29 30) to the north, and longitude (45 45) to the east. It should be noted that the State of Kuwait includes nine islands, only one of them is inhabited, and the country occupies, within its geographical borders, an area estimated at 17,818 km2, and with this area it ranks 157th among the countries of the world in terms of geographical area


Climate of the State of Kuwait

By virtue of its location in the desert geographic region, the State of Kuwait enjoys a continental climate. Where warm weather prevails with rain at some times during the winter season, when temperatures range between 0 and -18 degrees Celsius, and this season witnesses irregular rainfalls; Where the quantities vary from year to year, but in the summer, hot and dry climates appear, during which dust storms may occur, and temperatures sometimes reach 50 degrees Celsius, and Kuwait experiences the fall and spring seasons within short periods of time the desert land may turn green with enough rain during the period between mid-March to the end of April, and the cold winds coming fromThe northwest winds during the winter, and it is hot during the summer, and these winds usually blow during the months of June and July, as well as the southeast winds with humid and hot characteristics, and the dry and hot winds of the south prevail during the spring and early summer.


Language and religion in the State of Kuwait

The Arabic language is the official language in the State of Kuwait. Most Kuwaitis speak Arabic, and the dialect of the people of Kuwait is represented by a mixture between the dialect of the regions of the Arabian Peninsula and the countries of Mesopotamia of the southern Euphrates branch, in addition to the presence of some English and Hindi words, and English is the most used foreign language in business, while Islam is considered Its official religion; The vast majority of the country's population is Muslim, and a proportion of Christians.


The currency of the State of Kuwait

The Kuwaiti dinar is the official national currency used in the State of Kuwait, and one Kuwaiti dinar is divided into a thousand smaller sub-denominations called (fils), and the Kuwaiti dinar has become the most valuable currency among all the world's currencies since 2019.


The most famous food and customs in the State of Kuwait

The food in the State of Kuwait is related to the culture of the country, and the traditional food is represented by several ingredients, such as: chicken, meat, fish, and lamb meat, with the presence of cooked rice, and eating is done using hands or tools designated for eating, and the quantities are usually large, and guests often participate With regard to food, and it is worth noting that the place where the guests meet is called (Diwaniyah). As for the meeting, it takes place once or twice during the week, in which various issues are discussed, along with the provision of meals and tea, and in what follows is a mention of the most important Kuwaiti popular food:


  1. Fish Mutabak: It consists of marinated fish and rice, usually zubaidi fish, known as (the national fish in Kuwait).

  2. Groats: It consists of mashed wheat grains with meat or chicken, spices, onions, and tomatoes.

  3. Harees: It is a dish that resembles soup, and is mixed with pureed meat.

  4. Al-Aqili Kesar: It is a type of sweets represented by a cake consisting of saffron, cardamom, rose water and toasted sesame.
